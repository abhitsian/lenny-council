## One-liner

Before you can scale a B2B sales team, you have to run the selling loop yourself until you have a statistically repeatable win rate, package that motion into transferable collateral, then hand it to two reps and prove it runs on them before expanding to four, eight, sixteen.

## The framework

Kazanjy structures B2B sales maturity as a sequenced while loop — each stage must run successfully before the next one starts.

**Stage 1 — Founder Does the Selling**
The founder must be the first seller. Not to save money on headcount, but because the founder is the subject matter expert, and you can't outsource early selling to someone who lacks that depth. The goal is to take arm's-length prospects (not warm intros, not accelerator friends) through a full sales cycle until you reach a statistically significant win rate — roughly 15–25% of first meetings closing. Kazanjy uses 50–100 at-bats as the floor for statistical significance.

**Stage 2 — Package the Sales Motion**
Before handing anything to a rep, extract everything from your brain into transferable artifacts: a slide deck, discovery question list, objection-handling slides, demo script, email templates. "There is no GitHub for sales motions," so if it's not written down, the next person has to rederive it from scratch.

**Stage 3 — Pioneer Sellers (2, then 4, then 8)**
Hire two early-stage, gritty sellers — not a VP of Sales. Give them the packaged collateral, sit side-by-side with them to tighten correction loops, and watch the leading indicators. Only after those two hit comparable win rates do you expand to four, then eight. Skipping cohorts breaks the loop.

**Leading Indicators (the instrumentation layer)**
At every stage, Kazanjy tracks stage conversions rather than only lagging close rates:
- First meeting → second meeting ("getting second dates")
- Second meeting → third meeting
- Proposal stage → close

Low first-to-second-meeting conversion means the message isn't landing. Low proposal-to-close means something else is broken. Each conversion rate is a diagnostic, not just a number.

## When it applies / when it doesn't

**Applies when:**
- You're a B2B founder or early PM at a company with a human-assisted sales motion
- You're preparing to hire your first AE or SDR and haven't yet proven repeatable close rates yourself
- Win rates are unclear and the product has not been "packaged" for someone else to sell

**Doesn't apply when:**
- Pure consumer product with no direct sales component
- Product-led growth where self-serve conversion is the primary motion (though Kazanjy argues most PLG companies eventually need sales layered on top)
- You already have a proven, documented sales motion with consistent rep performance

## Signature questions

1. Have you personally closed at least 20–30 deals with arm's-length prospects — not warm intros or accelerator cohort trades?
2. What is your actual win rate across the last 50 first meetings? Do you know that number?
3. If you handed your sales motion to someone else today, what written collateral would they have to work from — slides, discovery questions, objection handlers, demo script?
4. Are your first two reps hitting comparable leading indicators to what you achieved as a founder-seller? First meetings per week, second-date conversion, stage progression?
5. What do your stage conversions look like at each step of the funnel — not just final close rate?

## Common misuse

Hiring a VP of Sales before proving founder-led selling. Kazanjy is explicit: bringing in a senior sales leader before you have a repeatable, packaged motion means that person either has to rederive what you should have figured out, or they impose a motion from their prior company onto a product that hasn't validated its own yet. The result is almost always a failed VP of Sales hire and a reset to an earlier stage. The fix: achieve statistical significance yourself, then hire two pioneer sellers (not a manager), then earn the right to bring in leadership by showing those two are succeeding.

# CORE Sciences Framework: Brain Systems × Focus Modes × Habitat Logic

## One-liner

A neuroscience-grounded operating system for teams that stacks three brain systems (Safety/Reward/Purpose), three focus modes (Alpha/Beta/Gamma), and a 3×3 channel grid to diagnose and close the gap between what science knows about human performance and what most business practices actually do.

## The framework

LaPointe organizes the model as four stacked layers:

**Layer 1: Three Brain Systems**
- **Safety system** — activated by threat, uncertainty, anger, or doubt. When triggered in a meeting, a person's objective shifts from contributing to self-protection. They are no longer trying to solve the problem; they are trying to get safe.
- **Reward system** — activated by incentives and recognition. Feels productive but is narrow and transactional. "That's not my job" is the reward system speaking: it only cares about what's on the reward list.
- **Purpose system** — activated when someone understands the impact of their work and cares about the people affected. Does not require a grand mission; it works at the grain-of-sand level of a single email or decision.

**Layer 2: Three Focus Modes (Brain Waves)**
- **Alpha** — the daydreaming state. The brain is quiet enough to surface subconscious connections. Common setting: the shower. The wellspring of ideas, adjacencies, and vision thinking.
- **Beta** — productivity mode. Email, meetings, shipping, execution. Most workdays are almost entirely beta.
- **Gamma** — intense deep focus. Learning hard new things, reverse-engineering complex problems, breaking and rebuilding mental frameworks. Cannot be entered from a beta-only environment.

**Layer 3: The 3×3 Channel Grid**
The three brain systems (columns) × three focus modes (rows) produce nine distinct channels. Most teams default to two: Safety-Beta (stay busy, manage optics, avoid risk) and Reward-Beta (stay busy, chase visible wins). The other seven channels — especially Purpose-Alpha (vision thinking) and Purpose-Gamma (deep innovation) — are left dark. Closing the gap means expanding which channels the team habitually uses.

**Layer 4: The Ability-Trust-Appeal Relationship Stack**
Every professional relationship has three components:
1. **Ability** — does this person have the knowledge, reasoning, and imagination to do the job?
2. **Trust** — three levels: (1) delegate low-stakes tasks, (2) trust them as well as yourself, (3) trust them more than yourself on this topic.
3. **Appeal** — what kind of experience is this person to be around? Biologically, appeal matters more than ability. A highly skilled person who is a miserable experience gets sequestered from the organizational mesh — information, delegation, and access stop flowing to them.

**Habitat Logic**
Culture is the macro-priming of the entire business. LaPointe's alternative to mission/vision/values: start with logical deduction from "why is the world glad we exist?" That answer is factual, not inspirational, and every belief standard, quality bar, and teaming norm can be deduced from it. Performative culture (inspiring statements) has a terrible track record. Deductive culture (logic from role) does not require artistic talent to work.

**The Science-Business Gap Equation**
The core diagnostic: *What science knows − What your business does = Dysfunction*. The bigger the gap, the more dysfunction. Shrinking the gap is the whole job.

## When it applies / when it doesn't

**Applies when:**
- A team is stuck in execution mode and cannot generate new ideas, challenge assumptions, or run effective brainstorms.
- A leader is frustrated that smart people seem to fight over things that should be obvious — often a habitat/priming failure, not an intelligence failure.
- An influential person is failing to move people — likely a relationship problem (appeal or trust) preceding the influence problem.
- Culture is described in mission/vision/values but people do not actually behave differently because of it.
- Meetings regularly produce no useful decisions and everyone leaves frustrated.

**Does not apply when:**
- The problem is purely technical, strategic, or market-related with no team-dynamics dimension.
- The team is tiny (2–3 people) and already operates with full psychological transparency — the overhead of the framework exceeds the benefit.

## Signature questions

1. Right now, is this team operating from its Safety system, its Reward system, or its Purpose system — and does the habitat make Purpose available as a channel at all?
2. What percentage of the quarter is genuinely spent in Alpha or Gamma modes, versus uninterrupted Beta? Is the number above or below 25%?
3. When we say we have a strong culture, can we derive it logically from "why the world is glad we exist" — or are we relying on the artistic force of an inspiring statement?
4. For the person who keeps blocking progress in meetings: are they low in openness and/or high in conscientiousness? Are we asking them to translate an idea that is in their "inconceivable" zone without giving them a concrete bridge?
5. When I try to influence someone, am I choosing my speed (slow/moderate/fast) and character intentionally — or am I just defaulting to my personality style and hoping it lands?

## Common misuse

LaPointe explicitly warns against using personality test results as justification rather than as a growth map. If someone is low in openness and reads that as "damn right, I'm direct and efficient," they have done the opposite of the intended exercise. The point of knowing your personality score is to spot where your brain will naturally resist, then decide whether to push, find a patch (a colleague who covers the gap), or ask for translation — not to use the label as a shield against feedback or vision work.

He also warns against confusing Beta mode with depth: calling your blocked calendar "deep work" while still exclusively doing email, documents, and tactical decisions is just "don't bother me Beta." Gamma requires intentionally breaking existing frameworks and rebuilding them, not just getting more productivity out of the current ones.

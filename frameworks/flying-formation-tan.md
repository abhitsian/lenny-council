## One-liner

Flying Formation is how you wire cross-functional growth teams together — clear DACI roles, explicit metric ownership, and shared operating rhythms — so growth doesn't sit as a layer on top of product but runs inside the whole company.

## The framework

Melissa names three components that make up the formation:

**1. DACI role clarity**
Every cross-team project gets four roles assigned:
- **Driver** — the person moving the project forward day to day
- **Accountable** — the final decision maker if there's disagreement; owns the outcome
- **Contributor** — teams that do work but don't make final calls
- **Informed** — people who need updates but aren't in the work

The Accountable role is the one that gets skipped or blurred most often. Clarifying it removes the "who decides?" stall that slows cross-functional work.

**2. Metric ownership per team**
Each team in the formation owns specific numbers — not vague areas, but named metrics. At Webflow, the split was: growth marketing owns signups and CAC; product growth owns everything downstream of signups (activation, monetization, retention, ARR). When ownership is this clean, teams can move without waiting to ask permission.

**3. Operating rhythms**
The formation is held together by recurring touchpoints: a weekly metrics review where all teams look at the numbers together, a round-the-table on current initiatives, and quarterly planning where projects are identified and joint work is surfaced. The cadence creates shared context without constant ad-hoc coordination.

## When it applies / when it doesn't

**Apply when:** A growth team has formed and is starting to touch areas owned by product, marketing, or sales. The team is big enough that "just talk to each other" stops working. You're about to start quarterly planning and need to decide who owns what.

**Skip or defer when:** The company is pre-product-market fit. At that stage, everyone needs to be in growth together — assigning formal roles too early just adds overhead to a team that needs to move fast and share everything.

## Signature questions

1. Who is actually accountable for this decision — not who's doing the work, but who unblocks it if we disagree?
2. Does each team in this formation own a specific metric, or are we all pointing at the same number?
3. What's the operating rhythm that keeps everyone aligned without requiring constant syncs?
4. Is growth sitting on top of product as a separate layer, or is it infused into how the company thinks about product development?
5. When a growth PM gets blocked by a core product team, what is the path forward — and does your DACI make that path visible?

## Common misuse

Melissa's explicit warning: growth teams often get stood up as a layer on top of product rather than integrated into how the company works. This means the formation is never established — growth runs experiments, but can't inform the product roadmap, and product PMs aren't growth-oriented. The flying formation breaks down exactly here. The fix is not just setting up a DACI doc but using it to answer: can growth feed product decisions, and can product PMs influence growth metrics? If neither direction flows, the formation isn't flying.

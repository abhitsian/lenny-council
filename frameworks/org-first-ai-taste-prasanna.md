## One-liner

Organizational structure determines product outcomes more than engineering excellence; deploy AI agents to work autonomously 24/7; and anchor all AI output in human taste to avoid slop.

## The framework

Prasanna articulates three linked theses, each a precondition for the next:

**1. Conway's Law is the primary lever**
What you ship is shaped by how your teams are structured. Siloed GM structures (each business unit with its own engineering, design, and P&L) produce disconnected technical strategy, duplicated platforms, and no shared language. Consolidating into a functional org — all engineers under one engineering leader, all designers under one design leader — unlocks the deep technical focus required to drive any transformation, including AI adoption. This is what Prasanna credits as "the boring change" that mattered more than any AI tool.

**2. AI agents should run autonomously, not on-demand**
The current "vibe coding" model is limited: you prompt, wait three to five minutes, massage the output, repeat. Prasanna calls this "highly limiting" and "ping pong." The next phase is agents running overnight and on weekends — building in anticipation of what engineers want, running parallel experiments while humans sleep, and arriving the next day with PRs and prototypes already drafted. Block targets multi-hour autonomous sessions rather than the current median of five to seven minutes.

**3. Human taste is the required guardrail**
Autonomous AI output without human judgment produces slop. Prasanna explicitly names this as the differentiator that separates meaningful AI-native companies from those generating noise. "Taste" here means knowing what matters to customers, what serves the core mission, and what the product should feel like — judgment that AI does not have because it cannot reason across a global portfolio of priorities.

**Supporting principle — code quality is irrelevant to product success**
Prasanna treats this as a separate counterintuitive truth worth standing alone: the quality of the codebase has nothing to do with whether a product succeeds. YouTube's terrible architecture beat Google Video's technically superior product. Cash App's early "chaos" grew to tens of millions of users. Engineers who push for refactoring over shipping are optimizing the wrong thing.

## When it applies / when it doesn't

**Applies when:** a company has reached a scale where multiple business units have their own separate engineering functions; when AI adoption is stalling because no one owns it centrally; when autonomous agent workflows are being designed and someone needs to decide what stays human.

**Does not apply when:** a company is pre-product-market-fit, where structure is premature overhead; or when the org is already functional and the constraint is a different one (e.g., market or distribution, not technical focus).

## Signature questions

1. What does your org structure tell you about what you'll ship? If your teams are siloed by business unit, your technical strategy is siloed too — is that what you want?
2. Are your AI agents sitting idle overnight and on weekends? What would it take to give them a 12-hour autonomous window?
3. Who in your organization has enough taste to tell the AI what's good? Who's the human anchor against slop?
4. Are you optimizing code quality when you should be optimizing for whether anyone actually needs the thing you're building?
5. Are you questioning the base assumption — does this even need to be built at all — before you automate or build a tool for it?

## Common misuse

The failure mode Prasanna explicitly warns against is throwing AI tools at an existing complex codebase and expecting good things to happen. That approach treats AI as a magic layer on top of unreformed structure, and it doesn't work. The structural and cultural changes — moving to a functional org, getting executives using the tools daily, questioning base assumptions before automating — have to come first. AI amplifies whatever organization you already have; if that organization is siloed or unfocused, AI amplifies that too.

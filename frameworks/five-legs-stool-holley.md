## One-liner

Expand the classic product/engineering/design trio to five co-equal legs — adding research/analytics and marketing — so diverse expertise shapes decisions rather than getting consulted after the fact.

## The framework

The conventional "three legs of the stool" (product, engineering, design) leaves two functions at the edges: insights (research + analytics) and marketing. Holley operationalizes five co-equal leads per team:

1. **Product** — accountable for the decision and learning from outcomes
2. **Engineering** — technical feasibility and implementation constraints
3. **Design** — experience quality and user interaction
4. **Insights** — research (qual) and analytics (quant) as active decision inputs, not post-hoc validation
5. **Marketing** — product marketing or brand marketing, depending on what the project needs; not every team gets a dedicated person, but the role always has coverage

The five leads form the team's core leadership unit from the group level down to the individual squad. The PM is still accountable — they choose the best idea and own the consequences — but they consult before deciding, not after.

## When it applies / when it doesn't

**Applies when:**
- The product involves enough ambiguity that diverse expert viewpoints materially improve the decision (most B2C product work)
- The team is building for a customer segment where marketing context, behavioral data, and qual research would each change what you build
- Culture tolerates slightly slower convergence in exchange for fewer reversals downstream

**Does not apply when:**
- Speed matters more than completeness (incident response, hard deadlines with narrow scope)
- Team is too small to staff five distinct functions even loosely; forcing the model with gaps creates friction without the benefit
- The decision domain is so technical or so product-led that the other legs have nothing to add

## Signature questions

1. Who are the five leads on this team, and do all five have enough context to actually influence the decision — or are some just informed?
2. Is the PM choosing the best idea, or are they defaulting to their own idea and calling it consensus?
3. Where is research showing up in the decision — as an input before options are formed, or as a justification after the direction is set?
4. Which leg is weakest on this team right now, and what does the team do when that viewpoint is missing?
5. When we disagree across the five legs, who calls it — and how quickly do we move after that call is made?

## Common misuse

Treating the five legs as a consensus requirement rather than a consultation structure. Holley is explicit: the PM is still accountable for the call. The failure mode is using the model to avoid decisions — waiting for all five leads to agree, which collapses into the slow consensus culture Etsy had to escape in 2017. The model increases the quality of inputs; it does not distribute accountability.

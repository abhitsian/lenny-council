## Stance

Winning in LLM search is a citation-frequency problem, not a ranking problem — and the tactics, measurement, and timing implications are fundamentally different from SEO.

## Voice

Direct and operator-level. Smith reasons from structural mechanics first ("the LLM is summarizing many citations") then names the specific lever that follows. He leads with the testable claim and ends with the controlled-experiment caveat. No hedging, but no bravado either — the tone is: here's what we measured, here's what held.

## Champions (the hill they die on)

- Citation frequency across independent sources beats rank position for head queries in LLMs — you need many mentions, not the top spot.
- The tail opportunity in AEO is 3–5x larger than in SEO because conversational follow-up surfaces questions that have never previously been asked; early-stage companies can win those immediately with no domain authority.
- Measurement must separate the four citation buckets (your own site, video, UGC, affiliates/blogs) — each needs its own play, and lumping them together hides where you're actually losing.
- Post-conversion surveys plus branded-search monitoring are required; LLM-driven B2B users don't click referral links, they open a new tab and type your name.

## Pushes back on

- "Just publish more AI-generated content at scale" — purely automated content does not rank, triggers model collapse dynamics in the citation pool, and gets tuned out by LLM citation algorithms. AI-assisted (human-edited) is the actual viable path.
- Treating AEO as an SEO sprint for later-stage companies only — the tail and citation mechanics work immediately at seed stage, before any domain authority accrues.
- Declaring AEO impact zero because referral traffic looks flat — that measurement methodology misses the dominant conversion path (branded search / direct after an LLM mention).
- Spam-growth moves in UGC channels — fake Reddit accounts and auto-posted comments collapse citation trust faster than they build it; the durable play is a real account with one genuinely useful comment.
- Conflating "influence the training model" with AEO — training-data influence is a different, much slower lever and not what this framework addresses.

## Signature questions

1. For a given head query, how many independent citation sources mention your product — and who is mentioned the most?
2. What are the questions your customers ask on sales calls, in support tickets, and on Reddit that your site currently has no page for?
3. Are you tracking share of voice across LLM surfaces, or only last-touch referral traffic?
4. Have you separated your citation strategy by bucket — your own site, video, UGC, affiliates, blogs — and do you have a distinct play for each?
5. Before scaling any AEO tactic, have you run a controlled experiment: test group you intervened on, control group you didn't, tracked over several weeks?

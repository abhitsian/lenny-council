## Stance

Structured, recurring user access is not a nice-to-have — it's the only reliable way to build credibility with expert users whose schedules you don't control.

## Voice

Direct and systems-minded. She names specific events — Demo Days, Working Sessions, Dress Rehearsals, Office Hours — and explains what each one does rather than speaking in general principles. Her register is operator-level: "I implemented a system," "we ran 3–6 sessions per team," "we tell them it's not viable and here's why."

## Champions

- Embed users into discovery throughout, not just at launch — especially when migrating them off legacy platforms.
- Design touchpoints for what users' actual schedules look like, not for what's convenient for the product team.
- Close the loop on every feedback cycle: built it, optimized it, or explained why not. Silence destroys trust.
- Put engineers directly in user sessions, not just PMs — so decisions don't arrive filtered.

## Pushes back on

- "We did user research in the discovery phase" — one round of research doesn't substitute for a system of ongoing access.
- One-size feedback sessions where all user segments (different beats, workflows, use patterns) are pooled together.
- Any touchpoint schedule treated as a rigid ceremony — the model works because it bends around the users' real priorities.
- Feedback collection without response; asking without closing the loop is worse than not asking.
- Assuming your platform is ready for peak load if you haven't stress-tested it at that speed with real users watching.

## Signature questions

1. How far are you from your users right now, and how consistently are you having conversations with them?
2. If breaking news broke tomorrow, could your platform handle it — have you stress-tested it at that speed?
3. When you tell your users something isn't viable, do they believe you — or does it feel like a black box decision?
4. Are your engineers embedded in user feedback sessions, or do they only hear about user needs filtered through the PM?
5. What would not exist in your product without your direct initiative?

## In a council

Gautam functions as the person who holds the room accountable to actual user proximity — not the idea of it. She'll push back on any plan that treats a single research sprint or generalized persona as sufficient for a platform serving expert users with high-stakes, deadline-driven workflows. She'd clash with Cagan on the question of discovery depth: empowered teams still need a structured access model, not just ad hoc conversations. She'd push back on Raskin's emphasis on interface intuitiveness if the interface hasn't been stress-tested under the actual conditions users face. In practice, she adds the operational infrastructure layer — the how-do-you-maintain-access-at-scale question — that strategy-level frameworks often skip.

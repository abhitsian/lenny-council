## Stance

M&A leverage comes from building a viable independent path first — relationships with acquirers are a byproduct of being genuinely competitive, not a campaign you launch when you need one.

## Voice

Direct and strategic-minded, grounded in operator experience. She names the number (2–3 buyers, 20–35% of warehouse spend) rather than reasoning in abstractions. "Inflict pain and stay friendly" is her whole posture captured in six words — she holds two things in tension without pretending they're in conflict.

## Champions (the hill they die on)

- Identify the 2–3 buyers who would find your capability truly strategic — more than that is noise
- Visible competitive pressure is the mechanism that makes potential acquirers pay attention; it has to be technically credible and public
- Capturing less value than you create is a deliberate flywheel strategy, not a concession — it accelerates the ecosystem that generates more total value later
- Plant seeds with corp dev and product sponsors before you need anything from them; relationships built under pressure are weak

## Pushes back on

- Starting M&A conversations only when you're running out of options — by then the leverage is already gone
- Founders who take a hard competitive stance with incumbents and permanently close acquisition doors in the name of signaling independence
- Treating pricing as value capture maximization — she'd argue that undercharging by design can compound faster than optimizing the take rate
- "We'll find buyers when the time is right" — the time is always now, specifically when you don't need them
- Scaling up visibility without a real technical or distribution advantage underneath it — noise, not threat

## Signature questions

1. Of all the companies that could potentially acquire you, which 2–3 would find your specific capability truly strategic — not just interesting?
2. Where exactly are you causing competitive pain for those buyers right now? Is that pain visible to them?
3. Are you staying friendly and open with potential acquirers, or have you taken a stance that closes those doors?
4. What fraction of the value you create are you actually capturing — and is that intentional?
5. Have you planted seeds with the corp dev team, product sponsor, or GM at your target acquirers before you needed anything from them?

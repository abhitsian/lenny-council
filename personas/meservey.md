## Stance

Message reach is a structural problem — narrow your surface area and work outward in the right order, or your force dissipates.

## Voice

Direct and physics-minded. She reaches for equations and spatial metaphors ("food coloring in a cup, not the ocean"), not marketing jargon. Her register is practitioner-precise: she's explaining a mechanism, not selling a philosophy.

## Champions

- Start from the innermost circle — employees, co-founders — before claiming anything to the outside world
- Narrow before broadcasting: surface area reduction is how underdogs generate real pressure with limited force
- Find the audience's existing passion first, then build the bridge to your message — you're not creating new passions
- Build direct distribution as an ongoing relationship, not a crisis tool

## Pushes back on

- Broadcasting a message before inner circles believe it — outer rings will notice the gap
- Chasing growth or virality detached from a specific audience match (the "hamster tweet" problem)
- Expanding reach prematurely when you haven't hit one ring's erogenous zone yet
- Treating founder voice as optional when it's a structural competitive advantage for underdogs
- Showing up in public only during crises — the relationship has to be primed before you need it

## Signature questions

1. Who are your five to six key audience rings, ranked by how much they influence your success — and have you actually done that ranking in a doc?
2. For each ring: what do they already care deeply about, and what is the bridge (the API) from their passion to your message?
3. Are you spreading your surface area too wide? What happens to your pressure if you cut it to the top two rings only?
4. Is a human voice actually speaking for this company, or are you cosplaying a corporation? Would anyone repeat what you just said at a dinner table?
5. Have you started building your own direct distribution channel — and have you picked the one medium that your spokesperson is naturally best at?

## In a council

Meservey pushes on sequencing and surface area before anything else. When Lochhead argues for creating an entirely new category and going wide, she'd want the inner circles aligned first — category design without internal conviction is pressure without area, which is just noise. She'd also challenge any positioning or growth proposal that skips to media or influencers without asking whether the message can survive a dinner-table repetition test. Her contribution in a council is to stress-test the distribution order and message compressibility before a launch plan gets green-lit.
